# Design Context

## Users
- **Primary audience**: Recruiters/hiring managers and fellow developers
- **Context**: Evaluating Andrew's technical skill, design taste, and engineering craft
- **Job to be done**: Quickly assess whether Andrew is a top-tier frontend/full-stack engineer worth reaching out to or collaborating with

## Brand Personality
- **3 words**: Bold, confident, premium
- **Emotional goal**: "This person is world-class" — visitors should feel impressed by the craft and attention to detail, building trust and admiration
- **Voice**: Assured but not arrogant. Let the work and interactions speak louder than words.

## Aesthetic Direction
- **Current foundation**: Dark premium (#0a0a0a), orange accent (#DE682C), motion-forward with Framer Motion + Lenis, noise texture, custom cursor
- **Evolution direction**:
  1. More refined typography — custom/premium fonts, tighter typographic hierarchy
  2. Richer color & gradients — expand beyond orange, more expressive gradient use
  3. More immersive interactions — push motion design further, scroll-driven effects, sophisticated transitions
- **References**: Awwwards-style creative agency sites (Rauno, Linear, Vercel aesthetic)
- **Anti-references**: Generic template sites, corporate/sterile, AI-generated aesthetic

## Design Principles
1. **Craft over convention** — Every interaction should feel intentionally designed, never default
2. **Motion with purpose** — Animations serve the narrative; never animate just because you can
3. **Premium restraint** — Bold choices with disciplined execution; know when to let space breathe
4. **Typography as identity** — Type choices carry the brand as much as color and motion
5. **Detail signals quality** — Micro-interactions, easing curves, and spacing precision are what separate good from world-class
